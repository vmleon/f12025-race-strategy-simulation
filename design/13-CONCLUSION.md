# Conclusion

This project set out to answer a deceptively simple question — _when should a driver pit, and what will it cost or gain them?_ — inside an environment whose governing physics are deliberately hidden. The EA Sports F1 2025 title exposes a rich telemetry stream ([Chapter 2](02-F1.md)) but no model of how that telemetry is produced; tyre degradation, fuel effect, pit-loss, and the systematically different treatment of AI and player cars are all proprietary. The work therefore became, at its core, an exercise in black-box system identification: reconstructing an unknown generative process from nothing but its observable output, and then using that reconstruction to reason forward about events that have not yet happened. Framing the problem this way is what gives the result its significance beyond the game. The same shape — ingest a high-frequency signal, fit interpretable models to it under uncertainty, and project probable futures — recurs across motorsport analytics, industrial telemetry, and any setting where a system must be understood empirically because it cannot be opened up and read directly.

The pipeline built to serve that question is the project's most concrete contribution. A 20 Hz UDP byte stream is parsed, condensed through a snapshot-on-transition strategy that records state only at sector boundaries rather than persisting every packet, and committed to a schema designed around the per-sector granularity that the analysis demands ([Chapter 4](04-DATABASE_DESIGN.md)). On top of that data, a calibration stage fits a per-car pace baseline together with a small set of physically meaningful coefficients, separated by physics regime and guarded by per-regime outlier detection ([Chapter 5](05-CALIBRATION.md)); a Monte Carlo engine ([Metropolis & Ulam, 1949](10-REFERENCES.md#metropolis1949)) then propagates thousands of race continuations at sector resolution to produce not a single prediction but a distribution of finishing positions ([Chapter 3](03-MONTECARLO.md)). The deliberate choice of per-sector over per-lap modelling, and of dual calibration over a single averaged model, reflects a recurring methodological stance throughout the work: resolve phenomena at the level where they actually occur — DRS zones, dirty air, overtakes, the divergence between scripted AI pace and simulated player physics — rather than at the level that is merely convenient to compute.

Turning that statistical output into something a driver can act on required a second, quieter achievement: integrating a genuinely heterogeneous system into a coherent whole ([Chapter 6](06-INTEGRATION.md)). A framework-free Java ingestion server, a Spring Boot orchestration layer, Python services for fitting and simulation, an Angular observability portal, and a native iOS client are bound together not by brittle point-to-point calls but by transactional message queues in the database ([Oracle Corporation, 2025](10-REFERENCES.md#oracle-txeventq)), which decouple component lifecycles and provide reliable delivery across languages and runtimes. The decision to keep ingestion as plain Java while embracing a framework where orchestration justified it — argued explicitly rather than assumed ([Chapter 7](07-ARCHITECTURE_ANALYSIS.md)) — is representative of the engineering judgement the project demanded: matching each tool to the constraint that actually bound it, and resisting abstraction until a real requirement called for it.

The most recent strand of the work, the natural-language race-engineer renderer, illustrates how the same evidentiary discipline was extended into machine learning. Rather than selecting a language model by reputation, an offline benchmark was constructed to run real radio messages through competing models, score them with an independent multi-judge panel ([Zheng et al., 2023](10-REFERENCES.md#zheng2023)), and weigh quality against the latency budget that live delivery imposes ([Chapter 12](12-RADIO_LLM_EVALUATION.md)). The selection that emerged — and the reasoning behind it — matters more than the specific model chosen: it required recognising that an aggregate quality score can actively mislead, because an equally-weighted mean lets stylistic brevity mask the one property that is non-negotiable for fact-bearing radio, namely faithfulness to the underlying numbers. Choosing the model that best preserved facts within a viable latency window, and being able to justify that choice from data rather than intuition, is exactly the kind of transparent, reproducible model-selection practice that the broader field increasingly asks for.

Equally important is what the project chose _not_ to claim. The catalogue of open questions ([Chapter 9](09-CHALLENGES.md)) — the multicollinearity that entangles fuel and tyre effects within a stint, the untested additive-versus-multiplicative form of the lap-time model, the unverified determinism of the game's physics, the empirical uncertainty of the dirty-air curve — is not a list of failures but a deliberate map of the boundary between what the data could already support and what it could not yet decide. Stating those limits precisely, defining the experiment that would resolve each one, and committing to conservative defaults until evidence arrives, is the difference between a system that merely runs and one whose conclusions can be trusted and falsified. That posture, more than any single component, is what the process taught.

Taken together, the project demonstrates that a complete, data-driven decision-support system can be reverse-engineered from an opaque real-time source and delivered, end to end, from raw bytes to a spoken instruction — and that doing so credibly depends less on any individual technology than on the consistency of the reasoning applied at each step: model phenomena where they occur, decouple what changes independently, select components from evidence, and be explicit about the edge of what is known. As a proof of concept it does not settle the underlying research questions it raises, but it builds the instrument capable of answering them, and in doing so it establishes both a working artefact and a defensible methodology on which further investigation can proceed.
